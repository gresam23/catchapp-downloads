⚠️ Please read before you install

CatchApp connects to WhatsApp using an unofficial method — WhatsApp doesn't offer a way for outside apps to read a personal account's group chats, so this is the only way something like CatchApp can work.

WhatsApp's own Terms of Service prohibit this. Using CatchApp means WhatsApp could suspend or permanently ban the account it's connected to, at any time, without warning, entirely at WhatsApp's discretion.

CatchApp only reads your chosen groups — it never sends bulk or unsolicited messages, and never messages people who haven't already messaged in that group. Most reported bans involve high-volume automated messaging, a very different use. We believe this makes the risk lower than a typical bulk-messaging tool — but there's no public data proving read-only use is safe, and this is our own judgment, not a guarantee.

Use CatchApp entirely at your own risk, on your own WhatsApp account.

Install CatchApp on Mac

About three minutes. No technical knowledge needed.

Before you start

CatchApp needs the Google Chrome browser installed on your Mac (it uses it quietly in the background to talk to WhatsApp). If you don't have Chrome, download it first from google.com/chrome — normal install, nothing special.

Step-by-step

  1. Download the Mac installer. On the downloads page, click the newest file ending in -arm64.dmg. (CatchApp supports Macs from 2021 or later — Apple Silicon.)
  2. Open the downloaded file. Click it in your browser's downloads list (or find it in your Downloads folder and double-click). A small window opens.
  3. Drag the CatchApp icon onto the Applications folder shown in that window. That's the "install".
  4. Open your Applications folder (Finder → Applications) and double-click CatchApp.
  5. First launch only: a box will say the app couldn't be verified. Click Done (NOT "Move to Trash").
  6. Open menu → System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down to the Security section — you'll see "CatchApp" was blocked to protect your Mac — and click Open Anyway.
  7. One more confirmation box appears — click Open Anyway again (your Mac may ask for your fingerprint or password). This whole dance happens once; afterwards CatchApp opens like any other app.
  8. You may see one more box titled "Allow the applications below to update or delete other applications," listing CatchApp — switch it on. This lets CatchApp install its own updates later without you doing anything.
  9. CatchApp opens and walks you through its own setup (terms, activation code, connecting WhatsApp).

Keep it running

CatchApp only reads messages while it's actually running. Keep the computer switched on and connected to the internet. If the Mac goes to sleep or shuts down, CatchApp pauses until you wake it and reopen the app — nothing is lost, it just catches up once it's running again.

About the "couldn't be verified" notice

Why it appears: Apple shows this for apps whose maker hasn't (yet) enrolled in Apple's paid developer program. It does not mean a virus was found. CatchApp is in its testing phase. The System Settings → Open Anyway route is Apple's own official way to open such apps.

If you ever see "CatchApp is damaged and can't be opened" instead, you have an old copy from before 2 July 2026 — delete it and download the current version, which fixes this.

Updates on Mac

For now, Mac copies don't update themselves (that needs Apple's paid developer membership, coming later). When a new version is announced, just download the new .dmg from the same downloads page and drag it to Applications again — your settings and WhatsApp connection are kept.
