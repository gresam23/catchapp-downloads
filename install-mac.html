Install CatchApp on Mac

About three minutes. No technical knowledge needed.

Before you start

CatchApp needs the Google Chrome browser installed on your Mac (it uses it quietly in the background to talk to WhatsApp). If you don't have Chrome, download it first from google.com/chrome — normal install, nothing special.

Step-by-step

  1. Download the right Mac installer. On the downloads page there are two Mac files: pick …-arm64.dmg if your Mac is from 2021 or later (Apple Silicon), or …-x64.dmg for older Intel Macs. Not sure? Click the menu (top-left) → About This Mac: "Apple M1/M2/M3/M4" means arm64; "Intel" means x64.
  2. Open the downloaded file. Click it in your browser's downloads list (or find it in your Downloads folder and double-click). A small window opens.
  3. Drag the CatchApp icon onto the Applications folder shown in that window. That's the "install".
  4. Open your Applications folder (Finder → Applications) and find CatchApp.
  5. Important — first launch only: don't double-click. Instead, hold the Control key and click the CatchApp icon (or right-click it), then choose Open from the little menu.
  6. A box appears saying the app is from an "unidentified developer" — click Open again. This happens once; afterwards it opens like any other app.
  7. CatchApp opens and walks you through its own setup (terms, activation code, connecting WhatsApp).

About the "unidentified developer" notice

Why it appears: Apple shows this for apps whose maker hasn't (yet) enrolled in Apple's paid developer program. It does not mean a virus was found. CatchApp is in its testing phase. The Control-click → Open trick is Apple's own official way to open such apps.

Updates on Mac

For now, Mac copies don't update themselves (that needs Apple's paid developer membership, coming later). When a new version is announced, just download the new .dmg from the same downloads page and drag it to Applications again — your settings and WhatsApp connection are kept.
