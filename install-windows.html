⚠️ Please read before you install

CatchApp connects to WhatsApp using an unofficial method — WhatsApp doesn't offer a way for outside apps to read a personal account's group chats, so this is the only way something like CatchApp can work.

WhatsApp's own Terms of Service prohibit this. Using CatchApp means WhatsApp could suspend or permanently ban the account it's connected to, at any time, without warning, entirely at WhatsApp's discretion.

CatchApp only reads your chosen groups — it never sends bulk or unsolicited messages, and never messages people who haven't already messaged in that group. Most reported bans involve high-volume automated messaging, a very different use. We believe this makes the risk lower than a typical bulk-messaging tool — but there's no public data proving read-only use is safe, and this is our own judgment, not a guarantee.

Use CatchApp entirely at your own risk, on your own WhatsApp account.

Install CatchApp on Windows

Takes about two minutes. No technical knowledge needed.

Step-by-step

  1. Download the installer. Click the newest file ending in .exe on the downloads page (it's called something like CatchApp Setup 0.1.0.exe). Your browser saves it to your Downloads folder.
  2. Open the file you downloaded. Click it in your browser's download list, or open your Downloads folder and double-click it.
  3. If a blue box appears saying "Windows protected your PC" — don't worry. This is a standard notice for new apps that aren't yet registered with Microsoft. It appears once. See the picture guide below.
  4. In that blue box, click the small More info link.
  5. Then click the Run anyway button that appears.
  6. That's it — the installer runs by itself. After a few seconds CatchApp opens in its own window, and you'll find a CatchApp icon on your desktop and in the Start menu.

About the blue "Windows protected your PC" notice

Why it appears: Microsoft shows this for any new app whose maker hasn't (yet) bought a Microsoft publisher certificate. It does not mean a virus was found. CatchApp is in its testing phase, so we haven't purchased the certificate yet.

The blue box has a title "Windows protected your PC" and one button: Don't run. The trick everyone misses: click the faint "More info" link just under the text — only then does the Run anyway button appear. Click that and you're through.

You'll only see this the first time you install. Updates install themselves quietly.

After installing

CatchApp starts with a short setup: accept the terms, enter your activation code, and connect your WhatsApp by scanning a QR code with your phone. The app walks you through each step.

Keep it running

CatchApp only reads messages while it's actually running. Keep the computer switched on and connected to the internet. If the computer goes to sleep or shuts down, CatchApp pauses until you wake it and reopen the app — nothing is lost, it just catches up once it's running again.
